# Day 1 Learnings

Today, I learned the foundational concepts of Salesforce and Customer Relationship Management (CRM).

## Key Learnings

1. Salesforce is a cloud-based CRM platform used by businesses to manage customer relationships and business operations.

2. CRM (Customer Relationship Management) helps organizations store, manage, and analyze customer information efficiently.

3. Salesforce centralizes customer data, sales processes, support activities, and automation within a single platform.

4. Core CRM concepts in Salesforce include:
   - Leads
   - Accounts
   - Contacts
   - Opportunities

5. In Salesforce:
   - Objects are similar to database tables.
   - Fields are similar to columns.
   - Records are similar to rows in a table.

6. Salesforce provides both standard objects and custom objects for managing business data.

7. A Salesforce Administrator is responsible for configuring, maintaining, and managing the Salesforce environment.

8. A Salesforce Developer builds custom functionalities using technologies such as Apex, Lightning Web Components (LWC), and Visualforce.

9. Salesforce can be used to develop real-world business applications such as:
   - College Management Systems
   - Inventory Management Systems
   - Vendor Management Applications
   - Customer Support Portals

10. Understanding data modeling and relationships between objects is an important part of Salesforce development.

---

## Real-World Understanding

Salesforce helps organizations improve productivity by bringing customer management, sales tracking, service operations, reporting, and workflow automation into a single integrated platform.

It is widely used across industries because it supports scalability, customization, cloud accessibility, and business process automation.
